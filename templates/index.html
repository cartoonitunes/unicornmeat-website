The Unicorn Meat Story üçñ

Unicorn Meat is a historic Ethereum meme token, created by Ethereum's Alex Van de Sande in March 2016 as part of a demonstration of token mutations, DAOs, bribable voting, and on-chain fun. A month after the Ethereum Foundation created Unicorns ü¶Ñ as an offering in return for anyone who donated 2.014 ETH to the Ethereum Tip Jar, Alex devised what was conveyed as an April Fool's joke ‚Äì a Unicorn Meat token and a Unicorn Meat Grinder Association contract allowing any Unicorn holder to grind their Unicorns for Unicorn Meat, as well as make proposals to change how the operation functioned.

A week after the announcement, Piper Merriam, a core developer of Ethereum, used the proposal and voting mechanism to take control of the contract and take 5 ETH for themself. In 2025, an Ethereum wanderer followed Piper's footsteps and passed a proposal to transfer the Unicorn Meat contract ownership to themself. A once infinitely mintable historic meme relic now has a finite supply and is wrapped to be tradable and owned by anyone!

Frequently Asked Questions ü§î

  • Is this the real historical Unicorn Meat?

    Yes! This is the same Unicorn Meat that was created in March 2016 by Alex Van de Sande. The contract was acquired through the Unicorn Meat Grinder Association contract. The supply was wrapped in a tradable modern ERC-20 token.

  • Can I unwrap Wrapped Unicorn Meat?

    Yes! Anyone can freely wrap and unwrap Unicorn Meat between the original 2016 contract and the wrapper. We have added buttons in this website to make it easy, but you can also do so directly on the wrapper contract.

  • Why is Unicorn Meat significant?

    Unicorn Meat is a historic token created by Ethereum leader Alex Van de Sande. It was created as an on-chain April Fools joke, allowing anyone to grind their Unicorn for Unicorn Meat. This was perhaps the first demonstration of a mutation of a token. Unicorn Meat is one of the few tradable tokens that was created by the Ethereum team.

  • Why was Unicorn Meat wrapped?

    The original Unicorn and Unicorn Meat contracts have slight deviations from the modern ERC-20 standard. These deviations are not supported by Decentralized or Centralized Exchanges. Wrapping the Unicorn Meat contract allows it to be used on modern exchanges. That said, those that want to hold the original coin can simply unwrap their wrapped supply.

  • Why acquire Unicorn Meat and make it tradable?

    Beyond the intrigue of acquiring ownership of a coin created by the Ethereum Foundation ‚Äî and the potential monetary upside ‚Äî there are several reasons this move made sense. The Unicorn Meat contract was claimed through a DAO proposal that reached quorum. Anyone holding just one Unicorn could have done the same ‚Äî and eventually, someone would have.
    Both the Unicorn Meat Grinder Association and the Unicorn Meat contract had been dormant for years. The Grinder Association saw only 23 transactions from a handful of addresses, with the last in September 2017. The Unicorn Meat contract itself had just 7 transactions, ending in December 2016.
    Some may cry foul ‚Äî and that‚Äôs okay. But hopefully, more will appreciate the chance to own a piece of Ethereum history and enjoy the fun that lies ahead.

  • Why renounce the contract?

    As long as the Unicorn Meat Grinder Association is not the owner of the Unicorn Meat contract, the unused Unicorn Grinder will not be functional. In order to make Unicorn Meat tradable, it's important to limit the ability to create more tokens to avoid meatflation. Grinding Unicorns yielded between 1,000 to 103,300 Uncorn Meat, using a pseudo-random block based algorithm that is easy to game to ensure you get 103,300 Unicorn Meat. 2,717 * 103,300 = 280,766,100 Unicorn Meat. 100,000,000 Unicorn Meat felt right.
